Another Place to Write and Not Write

February 25, 2022

I have a tendency to encapsulate myself on the Internet behind multiple names, accounts, email addresses, and identities. I wouldn’t say I always put an unnecessary amount of effort into keeping them apart. But the multiple aspects thing has always been a part of me.

I was known for changing my screen names too often even back in the AOL days before any of us had considered the the actual security implications of the modern web; so it’s just a part of who I am and not always only paranoia or a tendency towards dissociation. Though, sometimes both of those things are true. I thing especially the last bit.

This is probably the only page that I have used a real picture of myself on the Internet since probably around 2015 or so. Back then, I had plenty of social media accounts filled with pictures and other confessions of myself just like anyone else. And I still use the Internet a lot, but I find it a far less comfortable place to be honest than I used to.

Most of that comes in the form of starting blogs that I want to try writing in but ultimately won’t. And when I do, those pages will be up only long enough to be forgotten, found again, and then replaced by another page with another name and another theme to play with.

Why Do I Delete the Pages that I Write?

I didn’t always do it. Back in those AOL Instant Messenger days, I also used to have a LiveJournal that I kept up with writing regularly for most of my young adulthood. I would go as far as to say that it was at some point mostly a habit. It was just a means of coping with my overactive, obsessive mind. But as a teenager, I believed that I was a good at writing. There were some small amount of people back then who would have agreed in some degree.

Sure, the Internet was just different back then, and maybe it was a lot easier to just let myself be revealed. After all, only nerds like people on LiveJournal and other sorts of niche things were even using it regularly. But think more than anything, I realized that I’m not a good writer.

Writing is as characteristic of my mental condition. Whether or not some people might have enjoyed it isn’t the point. I know that when I was doing it seriously all the time that some people did. But I was never in control or able to to manifest exactly what I wanted. I was just compulsively engaging in a mannerism that was a part of me. Like minnows swimming upstream.

As I got older and the Internet changed — LiveJournal was taken over by Russians — and no one was left on the Internet who wanted to talk about their most private thoughts semi-publicly anymore. And at this point, I don’t even blame them. This is incredibly difficult for me.

The more I write, the more things that I have written accumulate and reveal progressively more things about who I am deep down — what issues I struggle with. It becomes apparent that for as passionate as I may have seemed to be, I suddenly become possessed by the spirit of another, who thinks about other things.

Those different people have had a a diverse amount of different types of blogs (among other things…) and such — about different subjects that I have started and and run away from and started over again a million times before, each time very differently written without connection to any others. The only connections sometimes is that the persons writing it actually are a person like it or not, and the way they write is very characteristic of him.

The reason why is because he is not able to stop that from happening as he writes. He simply does so.

They’re Not All Deleted Actually

In fact most of them are not actually deleted. While more than a few things have been lost to the ages, forgotten or lamented on ancient hard drives that will never spin another day, most are not actually worth thinking about very much. Many things I’ve lost I probably wouldn’t remember if you put them in front of me anyway. but the things I have had for a long time I do remember for that reason. They’re not mostly deleted — just taken out of front facing places and left unmaintained. Usually it’s difficult to think of these writings as having come from or having anything to do with the present me.

A large part of why they are phased out is because they become less relatable for me to continue working on sometimes. I will remember the writer as if he were a character who I read many serial entries about in a long running fiction. I do actually know would be a fantasy if actually could derealize enough to not be the person that I have been… — and moreover, the person who I still am — in spite of the jarring contrast between these characters.

You could easily trace it back through the wayback machine if you were inclined enough. And you’d get an idea what I was writing about then wasn’t that bold and dangerous of a subject to try out as a wordsmith.

I’m afraid that is a subject I don’t have time to start, but overall, writing about programming projects is hardly the most shamefully difficult of honest topics where I have revealed the most about myself and feel the most insecure.

…or…

Maybe it is actually one of the most difficult things for me. Maybe like being a writer all those years ago, I have realized again that I am also not a good programmer either.

I am merely a strange obsessive man who dwells on the Internet. And I’m a good enough — or so I believe for now — liar to have gotten away with the delusion.
